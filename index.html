Diana Frances Spencer

Queen of Hearts

Here's a time line of Diana Spencer's life:
  • 1961 - Diana is born at Park House, Sandringham.
  • 1969 - Her parents' divorce enables her to relate to other people who struggle with their family.
  • 1975 - At 14, Diana inherits the title 'Lady' when her father becomes the Earl Spencer. This title earns her the nickname of 'Lady Di'.
  • 1977 - Prince Charles meets Diana for the first time.
  • 1981 - Charles and Diana announce their engagement and announced their first child's pregnancy.
  • 1982 - Prince William is born at St. Mary’s Hospital. His full name is William Arthur Phillip Louis.
  • 1984 - Charles and Diana’s second son, Prince Henry (Harry) is born. His full name is Henry Charles Albert David.
  • 1987 - Diana is photographed shaking a HIV patient’s hand without wearing gloves, challenging public stigma towards the illness.
  • 1989 - During a 40th birthday party for Camilla Parker Bowles’ sister, Diana reportedly confronts Camilla about her affair with Charles.
  • 1992 - John Spencer (Diana’s father) dies of a heart attack. Diana refuses Charles’ offer of accompanying her back from their Austrian ski holiday, but the palace overrules her and he goes anyway.
  • 1992 - After 11 years of marriage, Charles and Diana separate. British Prime Minister John Major announces that the royal couple are separating "amicably". They begin fulfilling royal duties apart from one another.
  • 1995 - Princess Diana appears on BBC1 Panorama. She discusses her experience of post-natal depression, self-harm and bulimia. She refers to there being "three people in [her] marriage", referring to Charles' affair with Camilla Parker Bowles.
  • 1996 - Charles and Diana’s divorce is finalised. Diana loses the title of 'Her Royal Highness' but retains 'The Princess of Wales'. In doing so, she loses any future claims to the British throne.
  • 1997 - Diana hits headlines around the world for her powerful work once again, when she bravely walks through a live minefield in Dirico, south east Angola. 22 years later her son, Prince Harry, retraces Diana's steps on his royal tour of Africa.
  • 1997 - Diana dines with Dodi Fayed at Paris’ Ritz-Carlton Hotel after a trip to Sardina together. They leave in a limousine and are pursued by photographers on motorbikes. A chase is followed by a tragic crash in the Pont de l’Alma tunnel. The driver, Henri Paul, and Fayed die at the scene. Diana is pronounced dead shortly afterwards at Paris' PItié-St. Salpêtrière Hospital, aged 36.
  • 1997 - Diana is buried at her family estate in Althorp. Her funeral is watched by an estimated 2.5 billion people worldwide. An estimated 1 million people gather on the streets of London to pay their respects.
